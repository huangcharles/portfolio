Lightning

DICE! This simulates an electric field generated by a Van de Graaff generator that travels past a Faraday Cage. When the field strikes the surface of the Faraday Cage--made with a conducting material--charge travels along the surface and exits from another point on the surface. Since the interior of the cage is (relatively) insulating, the field does not enter the cage. An example of a Faraday Cage is a modern airplane; when lightning strikes the plane in mid-air, all passengers are safe because the current travels through the surface of the fuselage and does not enter the insulating cabin.
